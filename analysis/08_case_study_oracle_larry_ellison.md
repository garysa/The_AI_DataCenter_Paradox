# 08 — Case Study: Oracle and Larry Ellison

## "Would Larry Ellison Ask the Same Questions?"

Larry Ellison, Oracle co-founder and Executive Chairman, is arguably the most publicly
committed believer in the AI data centre investment thesis among tech's elder statesmen.
In January 2025, he stood beside Donald Trump, Sam Altman, and Masayoshi Son to announce
**Stargate** — a $500 billion AI infrastructure project. He has since committed Oracle to
$50 billion in capital expenditure for a single fiscal year.

This case study applies the paradox framework directly to Oracle's position:
**Can Oracle make money on its AI data centre bets before technology obsoletes, AI fails to
profit, or communities block it?**

---

## Oracle's Unique Position in the Paradox

Oracle is neither a pure-play cloud provider like CoreWeave nor a mega-hyperscaler like AWS
or Google. It sits in a distinctive third lane:

- **Legacy profitable database/ERP business** — providing cash flow and balance sheet
  strength, but declining as a share of revenue
- **OCI (Oracle Cloud Infrastructure)** — a late entrant to cloud that missed the first
  wave but is now growing fast by specialising in AI workloads
- **The Stargate anchor role** — Oracle is the physical infrastructure builder for the
  world's highest-profile AI investment project

This positioning gives Oracle specific advantages — and specific vulnerabilities — relative
to the paradox.

## The Numbers: Spectacular Growth, Spectacular Spending

### Revenue Growth

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Cloud Infrastructure (IaaS) revenue Q4 FY2025 | $3.0B (up 52% YoY) |
| Cloud Infrastructure Q2 FY2026 | $4.1B (up **68% YoY**) |
| GPU-related revenue growth | **+177% year-on-year** |
| Total company revenue Q2 FY2026 | $16.1B (up 14% YoY) |
| OCI margin improvement target | +30–40% over contract life |

### The Backlog — Where It Gets Extraordinary

Oracle's Remaining Performance Obligations (RPO) — contracted but not yet recognised revenue:

| Period | RPO |
|--------|-----|
| Q4 FY2025 | $138 billion |
| Q1 FY2026 (one quarter later) | **$455 billion** (359% surge) |
| Latest (Q2 FY2026) | **$523 billion** |

The explosive jump from $138B to $455B in a single quarter was driven almost entirely by a
**$300 billion, 5-year super-contract with OpenAI** for computing capacity. That one deal
represents 94.6% of the newly added RPO.

Oracle stock posted its **biggest one-day gain since 1992** on announcement. Because Ellison
owns roughly 41% of Oracle, the paper math briefly made him the **richest person alive**.

### The Cost Side

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| FY2026 CapEx plan | **$50 billion** (raised from $35B) |
| Free cash flow (trailing 12 months) | **-$13 billion** |
| Free cash flow (latest quarter) | -$10 billion |
| Debt on balance sheet | **>$90 billion** |
| Cash on hand | ~$10–11 billion |

Oracle's free cash flow turned **negative for the first time since the early 1990s** as a
direct result of its AI infrastructure buildout.

## Stargate: The Grand Bet

### What Is It?

**Stargate LLC** is a joint venture between OpenAI, SoftBank, Oracle, and investment firm MGX,
announced January 21, 2025 alongside President Trump.

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Total investment planned | $500 billion by 2029 |
| Initial committed capital | $100 billion |
| Chair | Masayoshi Son (SoftBank) |
| Infrastructure builder | Oracle |
| Primary compute tenant | OpenAI |
| Trump's executive action | Emergency declarations to expedite energy permits |

### The Abilene Anchor — Unprecedented Scale

The flagship Stargate data centre in Abilene, Texas:
- **450,000 Nvidia GB200 GPUs** — the largest single AI cluster anywhere on Earth
- **1.2 gigawatts** of total power capacity
- **1,000+ acres**, eight interconnected buildings
- Planned expansion to 5 new sites: Shackelford County TX, Doña Ana County NM, Lordstown OH,
  Milam County TX, and a Midwestern site
- Total planned capacity across Stargate: **~7 gigawatts** — enough electricity to power
  roughly 5 million homes

Oracle is also building more cloud infrastructure for **Microsoft** alongside the Stargate
commitments — making Oracle infrastructure for its own biggest cloud competitors.

## Applying the Three Paradox Clocks to Oracle

### Clock 1: Technology Obsolescence

**Oracle's exposure: HIGH — but partially mitigated by contract structure**

Oracle's Abilene facility is being built around **Nvidia GB200 GPUs** (Blackwell generation).
The next generation — Rubin (2026) and Rubin Ultra (2027) — will offer substantially better
performance per watt.

However, Oracle has structured its exposure through **long-term contracts with specific GPU
commitments**. The $300B OpenAI contract is priced for specific hardware. If that hardware
becomes less competitive, the contract terms don't automatically allow OpenAI to demand
next-gen hardware at the same price.

**Risk:** OpenAI's appetite for Oracle infrastructure may not renew at contract end if
B200-era facilities are outcompeted by next-generation Rubin clusters that Oracle's
competitors have built. Oracle has a **narrow window** to prove OCI performance and lock in
customer relationships before the next hardware generation resets the competitive landscape.

**Seeking Alpha described Oracle's capex commitment as an "AI Capex Blunder"** — arguing that
the hardware being purchased now will be obsolete before the contracts are fully earned, and
that Oracle is locking in debt that must be serviced beyond the competitive lifespan of the
infrastructure.

The counter-view: the **Abilene cluster's cooling infrastructure** (Oracle has specifically
designed for dry cooling and closed-loop systems) may be more upgradeable than typical
air-cooled facilities — reducing the physical plant obsolescence risk even if hardware is
refreshed.

### Clock 2: AI Profitability

**Oracle's exposure: CRITICAL — single-customer dependency**

Oracle's $523 billion backlog sounds extraordinary. The problem: **$300 billion of it is
from OpenAI** — a company that, as documented in Analysis 03, is currently burning cash at
a ~70% rate and won't be profitable until 2029–2030 at the earliest.

This creates a specific risk that most analysts are underweighting:

**If OpenAI fails, restructures, or loses its Microsoft lifeline:**
- The $300B contract could be renegotiated, reduced, or terminated
- Oracle's RPO would collapse from $523B to approximately $223B overnight
- The capex ($50B/year) already committed to build the Abilene infrastructure
  would still need to be serviced

**The Medium article titled "Oracle & OpenAI — Larry Ellison's $300B AI Fantasy?"** asks
the uncomfortable question directly: is a $300B contract with a company that loses $9B a year
actually worth $300B?

Oracle's defenders note:
- Microsoft is backing OpenAI — so by extension Microsoft's credit is behind the contract
- Even if OpenAI reduces compute usage, it would likely migrate to Microsoft Azure (which
  also uses Oracle infrastructure under separate contracts)
- The hyperscaler demand independent of OpenAI is real and growing

**Oracle expects OCI margins to improve 30–40% over the life of customer contracts** — this
assumes full utilisation, which depends on AI demand materialising as projected.

### Clock 3: Community and Regulatory Opposition

**Oracle's exposure: MODERATE — proactive community strategy, but Stargate's scale creates
new risks**

Oracle has taken a notably more proactive community engagement approach than some competitors:

- **Water**: Employs dry coolers and closed-loop chiller systems with **zero water wastage**
  in new facilities — a direct response to the opposition faced by competitors over water use
- **Energy costs**: Oracle funds all electrical infrastructure upgrades directly,
  **no cost to local ratepayers** — removes the most politically explosive community concern
- **Community investment**: $50 million committed to modernise Doña Ana County, New Mexico's
  water system (independent of data centre operational needs)
- **Sustainability**: Targets 100% renewable electricity by end of 2025
- **Energy partnerships**: Tapped Bloom Energy for power at key sites; pursuing SMR deals

**However, Stargate changes the risk profile dramatically:**

The 7 GW of planned Stargate capacity represents an energy demand equivalent to a small
country. Trump's use of **emergency declarations** to expedite energy permits bypasses the
normal community consultation process — guaranteeing opposition from communities that feel
overridden. Abilene, Shackelford County, and other Stargate sites are in Texas — a state
where grid reliability is already politically charged after Winter Storm Uri (2021).

The political protection of having presidential backing is double-edged: it may accelerate
construction in the short term, but it also makes the project a lightning rod for political
opposition when administrations change.

## Larry Ellison's Thesis: Why He's Betting the Company

At Oracle AI World 2025, Ellison laid out his worldview in 19 key points. His core
argument:

1. **AI is not optional** — every enterprise will need AI, just as every enterprise needed
   databases in the 1990s. Oracle, as the enterprise database company, is positioned to be
   the enterprise AI infrastructure company.

2. **OCI is a superior architecture** — Oracle built OCI specifically for large-scale AI
   clusters with RDMA networking, high-memory-bandwidth configurations, and tight integration
   with Oracle's database and applications stack.

3. **The enterprise moat is real** — enterprises running Oracle EBS, Fusion, or Autonomous
   Database will prefer Oracle cloud for AI workloads because of data gravity and integration.

4. **The backlog is the proof** — $523B in contracted future revenue represents demand that
   has already been signed, not speculative. It is "decades of guaranteed future revenue."

5. **He is #1 in AI data centres** — Ellison publicly declared Oracle will be No. 1 in
   cloud databases, cloud applications, and the construction/operation of AI data centres.

**The question is not whether Ellison believes it. He demonstrably does.
The question is whether the bet pays off before the three clocks expire.**

## The Oracle ROI Model: Best Case vs. Stress Case

### Best Case

| Assumption | Outcome |
|-----------|---------|
| OpenAI contract holds; $300B flows over 5 years | $60B/yr revenue from single customer |
| OCI margins reach 30-40% on AI workloads | $18–24B/yr operating profit from OCI alone |
| Enterprise customers migrate to OCI for AI + database | Cloud revenue reaches $166B by FY2030 |
| Stargate is operational and fully utilised | Oracle becomes largest single AI infrastructure provider globally |
| Hardware refreshed under multi-gen contracts | Obsolescence risk managed through contract renegotiation |

**In this scenario, Oracle's $50B/yr capex is justified and the ROI is achieved before
hardware becomes obsolete.** The 5-year OpenAI contract provides enough revenue runway to
service the infrastructure debt and establish the next-gen facilities.

### Stress Case

| Risk | Outcome |
|------|---------|
| OpenAI misses revenue targets, seeks contract relief | $300B RPO drops; Oracle cash flow stays negative |
| AI efficiency improvements reduce compute demand | OCI utilisation well below 100%; margins don't improve |
| Community/political opposition delays Stargate sites | Construction costs escalate; revenue delayed |
| Rubin generation makes B200 clusters obsolete faster | Oracle locked into depreciating hardware under $90B+ debt |
| Hyperscalers (AWS, Azure, GCP) undercut OCI on price | Oracle loses non-Stargate AI customers |

**In this scenario, Oracle's negative cash flow (-$13B TTM) is unsustainable.** The company's
$90B+ debt burden on an $11B cash position forces either asset sales, equity dilution, or
renegotiated debt — compressing future returns for current shareholders. Seeking Alpha's
"death cross" warning (January 2026) reflects exactly this risk.

---

## Verdict: Is Ellison's Bet Sound?

**Short answer: It's the most credible big bet in the AI infrastructure space — but it
contains a single catastrophic concentration risk.**

**Reasons it likely works:**
- Oracle has a profitable enterprise software business that generates ongoing cash flow
- The OCI architecture is genuinely strong for AI workloads (independent analyst assessments)
- Enterprise customers with Oracle databases have real incentive to keep workloads on Oracle
- The Stargate presidential backing provides a degree of regulatory protection
- Oracle's community engagement approach is better than most competitors

**Reasons it might not:**
- **$300B from OpenAI is the entire bull case.** OpenAI's profitability timeline (2029–2030)
  barely fits within the contract window. Any OpenAI distress propagates directly to Oracle.
- **$50B/year capex with -$13B free cash flow** is a narrow tightrope. One quarter of
  significantly missed cloud revenue growth and Oracle faces a credit market reckoning.
- **Rubin (2026) and beyond** will create customer demand for next-gen hardware in facilities
  Oracle is building right now. The upgrade cycle risk is real.
- **The Stargate timeline mismatch**: OpenAI break-even in 2029–2030 is the same timeline
  in which the Abilene facility's H200/B200 hardware will be entering its second-tier phase.

**The meta-question for Larry Ellison:**

In 1990, Larry Ellison transformed Oracle from a niche database company into the enterprise
software giant by betting — correctly — that every company would need a relational database.

He is making the same structural bet again: that every company will need AI, that AI will
run on GPU clusters, and that Oracle should be the infrastructure provider.

He may be right about the destination. The question, as this entire analysis documents, is
whether the path from here to there destroys enough investors and communities on the way
that the eventual winners look back on the journey as a cautionary tale rather than a triumph.
