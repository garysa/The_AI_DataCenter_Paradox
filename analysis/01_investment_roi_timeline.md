# 01 — Investment & ROI Timeline

## The Scale of the Bet

The numbers involved are staggering. Hyperscaler capital expenditure — the money spent building
and equipping AI data centres — is on a near-vertical trajectory:

| Year | Top 5 Hyperscaler CapEx (combined) |
|------|-------------------------------------|
| 2024 | ~$256 billion |
| 2025 | ~$443 billion |
| 2026 | ~$600 billion (projected) |

Individual company commitments for 2025 alone:
- **Amazon**: $125B capex (up 61% year-on-year), buying a 960 MW nuclear campus
- **Microsoft**: $34.9B in a single quarter (up 74% YoY)
- **Alphabet (Google)**: $91–$93B (revised upward three times during the year vs $52.5B in 2024)
- **Meta**: $70B — Zuckerberg explicitly said this "is very likely to be profitable"

McKinsey estimates **$7 trillion** in global data centre infrastructure CapEx by 2030.

---

## The Revenue Reality

Here is the core problem, articulated most clearly by Sequoia Capital's David Cahn:

> **"AI's $200B question is now AI's $600B question."**

The calculation:
- Nvidia's run-rate GPU revenue × 2 (total data centre cost estimate) × 2 (50% gross margin
  assumption for end-users) = **~$600B annual revenue needed** just to justify the hardware spend.
- Current AI-specific revenue generated by all these facilities: **~$25B in 2025**.
- The gap: **~$575 billion per year**.

Even with optimistic projections where Google, Microsoft, Apple, and Meta each generate $10B
annually from AI services, and Oracle, ByteDance etc. chip in $5B each, there remains a
**$500B+ annual gap**.

As of December 2025, Cahn's analysis confirmed: **the gap has not closed; it has widened.**

---

## Payback Period: How Long Does It Actually Take?

### Industry surveys (general AI investments)
- Most respondents reported satisfactory ROI within **2–4 years**.
- Traditional technology investment expectation: **7–12 months**.
- Conclusion: AI ROI takes **3–6x longer** than typical tech investments.

### Data centre-specific
- Facilities coming online in 2025 face ~$40B in **annual depreciation costs** (primarily GPUs
  and specialised equipment).
- Those same facilities generate **$15–20B in revenue** at current utilisation rates.
- To break even they need a **10× increase** in either utilisation or pricing — or both.

### The "J-curve" problem
AI infrastructure investment creates a classic J-curve: enormous capital upfront, revenue trickling
in slowly, with the break-even point potentially **a decade or more** away at current trajectories.

---

## Who Is Actually Generating Returns?

The picture is not uniformly bleak. There are tiered outcomes:

**Tier 1 — Hyperscalers with existing profitable cloud businesses:**
These companies (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud) can cross-subsidise AI infrastructure from their
existing profitable cloud operations. AWS is projected to surpass **$100B annual revenue** in
2025. Azure AI revenue alone exceeds **$10B/quarter**.

**Tier 2 — Pure-play AI cloud providers (CoreWeave, Lambda, etc.):**
These companies have no legacy profitable business to fall back on. CoreWeave's March 2025 IPO
illustrates the pressure: carrying **$12.9B in debt accumulated in two years**, the company
was forced to slash its valuation from $35B to $23B before going public.

**Tier 3 — AI software companies:**
OpenAI burns ~$9B on $13B revenue (70% burn rate). Anthropic burns ~$3B on $4.2B revenue
(also ~70%). Neither is profitable. OpenAI won't break even until 2029–2030; Anthropic projects
break-even in 2027–2028.

---

## Financing the Gap: A Red Flag

The top five hyperscalers raised a **record $108B in debt in 2025** — more than 3× the nine-year
average. They are increasingly relying on external financing beyond internal cash flow to fund
the build-out.

The market for Data Centre **Asset-Backed Securities (ABS)** has grown to over **$100 billion**,
packaging lease payments into bonds sold to institutional investors. This creates systemic risk:
if data centre values decline (due to hardware obsolescence or demand shortfalls), the equity
tranches of these ABS structures could be wiped out.

---

## Debt-Maturity vs. Hardware-Cycle Mismatch

Perhaps the most structurally dangerous issue:

- Lease terms: **10–15 years**
- GPU generation cycles: **18–24 months**
- Civil works (buildings, power infrastructure): **30-year lifespans**

A data centre built for 2023-era H100 GPUs could be **functionally obsolete by 2028** — but
still carrying multi-decade debt obligations. The building lasts 30 years. The reason for
building it lasts 3.
