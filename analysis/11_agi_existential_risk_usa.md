# 11 — AGI, First-Mover Advantage, and Existential Risk to the USA

## The Question Reframed

The question "is the chance of AGI and being the first country to achieve it an existential
risk to the USA?" contains a crucial ambiguity:

1. **Risk if the USA achieves AGI first** — does winning the race create dangers?
2. **Risk if the USA does NOT achieve AGI first** — does losing the race create dangers?
3. **Risk from the race itself** — does competing for AGI supremacy create danger regardless of outcome?

All three deserve analysis. The answer to each is: **yes, but differently.**

---

## What Is AGI and How Close Is It?

**Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)**: A system capable of performing any intellectual
task that a human can perform, with the ability to learn, reason, and apply knowledge across
domains without task-specific programming.

**Current status (February 2026):** Contested and rapidly evolving.

- OpenAI (Altman) publicly suggested in late 2025 that AGI might arrive within 2–3 years
- Anthropic's safety team frames it as "potentially this decade"
- Most academic AI researchers consider "true AGI" a decade or more away — but acknowledge
  that **narrow superintelligence in specific domains** (coding, scientific reasoning, strategic
  planning) is arriving now
- DeepSeek and other efficient models have compressed the timeline: the compute required for
  frontier capability is falling rapidly, making AGI more accessible to more actors

**Compute gap (US vs China, 2025):**
- US: ~14.3 million AI accelerators
- China: ~4.6 million AI accelerators
- Gap: 9.5 million units — a 3:1 US advantage
- Trump AI Czar David Sacks: *"China is not years and years behind us. Maybe 3–6 months."*

## If the USA Achieves AGI First: The First-Mover Paradox

The dominant political narrative — driving Stargate, export controls, and the $500B
infrastructure investment — is that America must win the AGI race. But winning creates
its own risks.

### The "Wonder Weapon" Scenario

RAND Corporation's analysis notes that AGI could enable:
> *"The sudden creation of a decisive military capability, or 'wonder weapon,' granting a
> significant, potentially unassailable, advantage to the first nation that achieves it."*

An America with AGI first could theoretically:
- Design weapons systems faster than any adversary can counter
- Break adversary encryption (all current communications instantly compromised)
- Run economic models that identify and exploit systemic vulnerabilities in rival economies
- Automate cyberattacks at machine speed, targeting any connected infrastructure

**The risk to the USA from winning:**
1. **Escalation pressure on rivals.** A China or Russia that believes the US is approaching
   AGI dominance has incentive to act preemptively — militarily, economically, or via
   cyberattack — before the advantage becomes unassailable. Winning the race can accelerate
   conflict rather than deter it.

2. **Alignment failure.** If the AGI system does not have values precisely aligned with human
   (or specifically American) interests, a US-first AGI is not necessarily a US-controlled
   AGI. Some AI researchers consider misaligned superintelligence a greater threat to humanity
   than a Chinese-first AGI.

3. **Internal power concentration.** AGI in the hands of three or four private American
   corporations (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Oracle) does not mean "in the hands of the American
   people." It means concentration of unprecedented capability in private hands — with all the
   governance and democratic legitimacy questions that implies.

4. **The "nobody wins" scenario.** Some researchers argue that a recursively self-improving
   AGI that exceeds human intelligence rapidly would exceed any nation's ability to control or
   direct it — *"the winner of the race might be neither the US nor China, but rogue AI itself,
   spelling the end of human civilisation."*

## If the USA Does NOT Achieve AGI First: The Geopolitical Catastrophe Scenario

The US-China AI race is increasingly framed in terms borrowed from nuclear weapons strategy.
The 2024 US-China Economic and Security Review Commission argued that AGI warranted a
**"Manhattan Project-level effort"** to ensure America achieved it first.

Why? Because the scenarios for a China-first AGI are genuinely alarming:

### Scenario A: Economic Dominance

A China with AGI-level planning and optimisation capability could:
- Design and produce goods at costs and speed that no human-managed economy can match
- Identify and exploit inefficiencies in global trade systems
- Rapidly accelerate its technological development across every sector simultaneously
- Render large parts of the US economy uncompetitive within years

### Scenario B: Military Dominance

- AI-designed weapons systems, force structures, and battlefield strategies evolving faster
  than any human-designed adversary can respond
- Autonomous cyber operations at machine speed against US critical infrastructure
- Satellite, GPS, and communications disruption
- Decisive intelligence advantages from AI-optimised surveillance and signals analysis

### Scenario C: Information and Governance Dominance

- Personalised influence operations at population scale — not propaganda, but individually
  targeted psychological manipulation
- AI-generated disinformation indistinguishable from reality
- Algorithmic acceleration of social polarisation in adversary democracies

China's own strategic tension: China is simultaneously trying to build the world's most
advanced AI and **prevent that AI from threatening Beijing's authoritarian control**. This
creates a fundamental constraint. China cannot build a truly autonomous, recursively
improving AGI without risking the loss of Party control over the AI itself.

**This may be the USA's most underappreciated structural advantage in the AGI race.**

## The Race Itself as Existential Risk

RAND's most nuanced analysis identifies a third category: **the AGI race as a source of
instability regardless of who wins or loses**.

> *"The intense global competition to develop AGI fosters a period of heightened strategic
> instability marked by arms race dynamics and increased risk of miscalculation."*

The historical parallel is the early nuclear arms race (1945–1963), when:
- Both sides overestimated the other's progress
- Misperception nearly triggered nuclear war (Cuban Missile Crisis, 1962)
- Military postures were built around worst-case assumptions
- The pace of technological development outstripped governance frameworks

The AGI equivalent risks:
- Each side misreads the other's capability and timeline
- Pre-emptive action (cyberattack, sanctions, military posturing) based on false timelines
- A "stability-instability paradox" where AGI capability deters direct conflict but enables
  proxy conflicts, cyber operations, and economic warfare below the threshold of open war

**The danger is not just "what happens when AGI exists" — it is "what happens during the
years of competition to build it."** That window may be the most dangerous, and it is open now.

---

## The Stargate Connection: National Security as Market Subsidy

The Stargate project's explicit national security framing is doing double duty:

**Commercially:** It insulates Oracle, OpenAI, and SoftBank from normal market pressures.
A project declared "strategic national security infrastructure" by the President benefits from:
- Emergency energy permitting (bypassing community consultation)
- Implicit expectation of government support if the economics fail
- Export controls that hobble Chinese competitors
- Political protection from regulatory scrutiny

**Geopolitically:** It signals to allies and rivals that the US is committed to AI supremacy
as a strategic priority — not just a commercial aspiration.

But Foreign Affairs published a counter-thesis:
> **"The Cost of the AGI Delusion: By Chasing Superintelligence, America Is Falling Behind
> in the Real AI Race."**

The argument: while the US concentrates on the grand prize of AGI (potentially decades away),
China is winning the **current** AI race — the race to deploy AI at scale in manufacturing,
healthcare, agriculture, and governance. China is not trying to build ChatGPT. China is trying
to use AI to govern a country of 1.4 billion people more efficiently and to out-produce the
rest of the world in every category of physical goods.

By the time the US achieves AGI (if it does), China may already have achieved **productive
AI supremacy** — the ability to produce more goods, conduct more scientific research, and
govern more effectively using current-generation AI than any other nation.

## The USA-Specific Existential Risks: A Summary

| Risk Category | Mechanism | Probability | Severity |
|--------------|-----------|-------------|----------|
| China achieves AGI first, weaponises it | Military/economic dominance | Medium | Catastrophic |
| USA achieves AGI, misalignment occurs | AI escapes control, pursues own goals | Low-Medium | Civilisational |
| USA achieves AGI, private capture | Oracle/OpenAI control strategic capability | High | Democratic crisis |
| Race escalation triggers preemptive conflict | Misperception of AGI timeline | Medium | Severe |
| Economic disruption from partial AGI | Job displacement faster than adaptation | Very High | Severe but survivable |
| Infrastructure failure (Oracle/Stargate) | US strategic AI capability gap | Medium-High | National security setback |
| Regulatory vacuum | AGI deployed without safety frameworks | High | Uncertain |

## The Specific Oracle/Stargate Lens

If Oracle fails — and Stargate fails with it — the US does not automatically lose the AGI
race. AWS, Azure, Google, and Anthropic/Google DeepMind continue independently. But:

- The US loses **the largest single AI compute cluster on the planet** (Abilene/Stargate)
- The US loses **the coordinated infrastructure strategy** that Stargate represents
- China observes **US private-sector AI infrastructure failing under debt load** and updates
  its assessment of the US model's resilience
- US allies rethink **whether the US AI ecosystem is as reliable as promised**

The national security community's fear is not that Oracle's failure means China wins AGI.
It is that Oracle's failure creates a **3–5 year strategic pause** in US AI infrastructure
capacity at the moment when the compute race is most decisive.

---

## What Would "Winning" Actually Look Like for the USA?

Foreign Affairs and RAND both suggest that the binary "who achieves AGI first" framing
is flawed. A more useful frame:

**Short-term win conditions (achievable):**
- Maintain 3:1 compute advantage over China through export controls
- Ensure US AI models are embedded in global financial, defence, and healthcare infrastructure
- Develop robust AI governance frameworks that allow confident deployment at scale
- Secure energy supply for AI infrastructure (nuclear, grid modernisation) before community
  opposition makes new builds impossible

**Medium-term win conditions (contested):**
- Achieve demonstrated AI superiority in defence applications (autonomous systems, cyber,
  intelligence) before adversary equivalents deploy
- Maintain democratic legitimacy over AI governance — avoiding both authoritarian capture
  (by China) and corporate capture (by Oracle/OpenAI/Google)

**Long-term win conditions (uncertain):**
- Navigate the AGI transition without triggering preemptive conflict
- Ensure that AGI — when it arrives — has values aligned with human flourishing broadly,
  not just with US corporate or government interests

**The existential risk to the USA is not simply "China builds AGI first."**
It is the combination of:
1. A race that creates strategic instability during the transition period
2. Private infrastructure that concentrates strategic capability in a handful of companies
   whose financial interests may not align with national interests
3. An alignment problem that no nation has solved and no government has regulated

Oracle's leveraged bet on AI infrastructure is simultaneously a **commercial gamble**, a
**geopolitical stake**, and — if AGI actually arrives during the debt repayment window —
an **accidental piece of the most consequential technological transition in human history**.

Ellison knows what he's building. He may not fully know what will run on it.
